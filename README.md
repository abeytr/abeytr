### 🩺 What I work on

I architect the **connectivity & serviceability platform** behind a fleet of medical
devices — the systems that let machines in hospitals around the world stay observable,
updatable, and secure. My job is to make that platform world-class *and* durable enough
to **outlast any single person**.

The throughline of everything I build: **observe before you automate, draw clean
contracts before you grant autonomy, and treat AI as a native enabler — not a bolt-on.**

```text
Cost center  ──▶  AI-native value driver

  Observability  ▸  Contract-Driven      ▸  Autonomous       ▸  Platform
  & Intelligence    Integration             Operations          Economics
  (observe)         (clean boundaries)      (humans escalate)   (value, measured)
```

### 🧠 How I think

I work mostly through **influence without authority** — no direct reports, just the
quality of the argument. So I've gotten a little obsessed with *thinking well* and
making good reasoning repeatable. That turned into a set of open-source
[**Claude Code skills**](https://github.com/abeytr/useful-skills):

- 🧭 **situational-awareness** — a context-switch recovery tool for anyone juggling parallel initiatives and fires
- 🔥 **grill-me** — stress-test a plan until every branch, dependency, and tradeoff is explicit
- 📐 **euclid** — harden an argument with the six critical-thinking habits Euclid's *Elements* trains, then run a hostile reviewer against *your own* favored answer
- 💡 **brainstorm** — a Socratic partner for finding the *simplest* resolution to a problem
- 🏛️ **system-architecture-review** — principled, adversarial review of an existing system's architecture

### 🔬 Currently exploring

- **Hardware-anchored device identity** — TPM-backed, sender-constrained API access (DPoP-style proofs) to defeat credential cloning on remote devices
- **AI-assisted SDLC** — making engineering org-wide faster without trading away rigor
- **Observability & SLOs as a foundation** — you can't automate what you can't see
